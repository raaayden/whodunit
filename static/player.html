❓ How to Play

3 rounds. Talk in person. Check your phone for clues each round. Vote for the killer at the end. This is a detective game — find the story that fits all the clues, not just the person who seems suspicious.

🏆 Win Conditions
  • Innocents win — majority of living innocents vote for the real killer at the end.
  • Killer wins — killer avoids getting majority voted.
  • Jester wins — if the Jester tricks the majority into voting for them, everyone else loses.

🃏 Roles

  • 😇 InnocentReceive genuine clues. Share them, misrepresent them, or stay silent.
  • 🔪 KillerSecretly queue one elimination per round. You have a prepared cover story. You do not know your accomplice.
  • 🤝 AccompliceYou know the killer. Protect them. You also have a prepared cover story.
  • ☠️ PoisonerAn accomplice who secretly corrupts one player's clues each round on their device. The victim never knows.
  • 🔎 InvestigatorYour Round 3 clue names exactly two suspects. One is the killer. Use it carefully.
  • 🍺 DrunkYou believe you're innocent. All your clues are false. You have no way of knowing.
  • 🧠 ParanoidYour gut tells you a specific person is the killer. Your gut is pointing at an innocent.
  • 🕵️ SpyOnce per game, secretly learn one other player's true role on your device.
  • 🃏 FoolYou think you're the killer but you're not. Play your role sheet exactly as written.
  • 🎭 JesterYou are neither killer nor innocent. Get the majority to vote for YOU. Act suspicious on purpose.
  • ⚰️ UndertakerAfter the Round 2 murder is revealed, you privately learn the victim's true role.
  • 🎭 RecluseYou are innocent, but every detection ability reads you as the killer. You will never be told this is happening.
  • 📋 AlibiEvery player has a private alibi for the time of the murder. Only the killer's is false.

🎭 The Bluff Register

  • Cover identity on your deviceThe killer and accomplices receive a fake innocent role to claim in conversation.
  • Their story is preparedDo not assume someone is innocent because they sound convincing.
  • Contradiction is a clueIf two people claim the same role, one is lying.

🕵️ Playing Like a Detective

  • You never have to reveal your roleClaiming to be "just a guest" when you're the Investigator is legal and sometimes the right move.
  • Clues form a webMultiple players saw the same events from different angles. Find the version where all the clues fit simultaneously.
  • Contradictions are evidenceIf two players describe the same event differently, one is lying. Figure out which version is consistent.
  • Role claims are not proofWhat matters is whether their claimed knowledge is consistent with what that role should actually know.

🎯 Private Objectives

  • Your personal goalSeparate from finding the killer — shown only on your device.
  • TypesProtect someone, steer suspicion, discover what someone knows, or manage your own standing.
  • Never revealedThe app does not track it. Whether you achieved it is between you and your conscience.

💀 Eliminated Players

  • You can still talk in personUse that.
  • You lose your final voteBut you can send one ghost clue from beyond the grave.

⚖️ Exile Vote (Round 2)

  • Nominate someone for public removalMajority agreement exiles them before Round 3.
  • Exiling an innocent helps the killerChoose carefully.

⚡ Crisis Dilemma (special games)

  • Everyone votes on a critical decision at Round 2Majority wins.
  • Dangerous option winsConsequences apply immediately — check your device.
